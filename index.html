Birds are bountiful in Baltimore City!

Baltimore City and it's surrounding regions are a haven for birds. For many migratory birds, it's waterways and rolling fields lined with brush and forest are an welcome refuge on thier long journey. Baltimore's many park spaces are year-round homes to generations of woodpeckers and songbirds. Even large birds like hawks, owls and vultures have taken up residence and call Baltimore's urban landscape home.

Birds in Wyman Park

In the fall, the Eastern Towhee descends to the brush for protection from predators. Sometimes popping up to say hello and show thier autumnal plumage.
Another bird dressed handsomely for fall is the Northern Flicker. The Northern flicker makes herself known with her arrow sharp keew and her red cap.
Baltimore's parks provide a permanent home to many resident Barred Owls. Ever watchful eyes look over the park's vistors and countless unfortunate rodents.
Red-tailed Hawk is Queen. This large raptor commands the attention of all nearby birds. Her arrival is often at the center of an accompanying sonic sphere of alert calls.
